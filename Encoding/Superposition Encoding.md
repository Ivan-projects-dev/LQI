#Quantum #Physics 
**Superposition encoding** attempts to leverage the parallelism of quantum states $>$ explicitly. Instead of rotating just Quantum states per feature, $1$ may distribute the info across [[Qubits]] to create $>$ complex superpositions. In some cases, this can resemble [[Amplitude Encoding]] but with structd or partial amplitude distributions that incorporate multiple features in a single multi-qubit superposition step. By carefully arranging gates (e.g., Hadamard transformations, controlled rotations), $1$ can encode patterns that might be $>$ discriminative for subsequent variational processing.
