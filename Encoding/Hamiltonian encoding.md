#Algorithm #Quantum #Math 
**Hamiltonian encoding** uses the idea that a [[Quantum state]] can evolve under a Hamiltonian $H(→x)$ that depends on the data $→x$. Formally, one prepares an init state $|0⟩$ (or another simple reference state) & applies $U(→x)=e−iH(→x)t$ for some evolution time $t$. The Hamiltonian $H(→x)$ is constructed so that its params incorporate classical data. Such encodings arise naturally in physics-driven problems or scenarios where time-evolution under a data-dependent Hamiltonian is readily implementable.
