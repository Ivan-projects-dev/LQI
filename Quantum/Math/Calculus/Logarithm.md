#Math  
If $c = \log_a b$ then $a^c = b$, for $a > 0,\ a \ne 1,\ b > 0$  
$\log_{10} a \equiv \lg a$  
$\log_e a \equiv \ln a$
$\log_a 1 = 0$
$\log_a a = 1$
$\log_a b^c = c \log_a b$
$\log_a bc = \log_a b + \log_a c$
$\log_a \dfrac{b}{c} = \log_a b - \log_a c$
$\log_b a = \dfrac{\log_c a}{\log_c b}$
$\log_a b = \dfrac{\log b}{\log a}$
