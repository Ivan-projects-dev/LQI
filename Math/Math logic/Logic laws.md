#Math 
1. Law of Non-Contradiction - $2$ contradictory statements cannot both be true simultaneously. Formally: $¬(A ∧ ¬A)$ - it is $false$ that $A$ & $not A$ are both $true$.    
- For example, a board cannot be both white & not white at the same time in the same respect.  
1. Law of Excluded Middle - For any proposition, either it or its negation is true; there is no third option. Formally: $A ∨ ¬A$ - either $A$ or $not A$ must be $true$.
This applies to classical [[Logic]] & rational discourse, though some paradoxes & philosophical problems challenge it.
