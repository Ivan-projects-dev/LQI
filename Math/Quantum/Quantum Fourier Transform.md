#Quantum #Math 
**Quantum Fourier Transform (QFT)** of state $|ψ⟩$ is given by $|ϕ⟩=N−1∑k=0yk|k⟩=1√NN−1∑k=0$ $N−1∑j=0e2πijkNxj|k⟩$,where $y_k$ is defined as above for $k=0,…,N−1$.

Hence for a basis state $|j⟩$ represented by the [[Vector]] $(x_0 x_1… x_(N−1))T$ where $x_j=1$ and the rest of the entries are $0$, its QFT is given by $1√NN−1∑k=0e2πijkN|k⟩=1√NN−1∑k=0ωjk|k⟩$, where $ω=e2πiN$. 

QFT is a linear transformation & represented by $N×N$ dimensional [[Matrix]].

Observe that QFT $|j⟩$ gives the $(j+1)$th column of QFT, where $j∈{0,…,N−1}$. 
QFT is a [[Unitary operator]].
