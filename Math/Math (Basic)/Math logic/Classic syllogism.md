#Math [[Logic]]
Classic syllogism:    
    - All humans are mortal.
    - Socrates is a human.        
    - Therefore, Socrates is mortal.
- This reasoning is **valid** because true premises guarantee a true conclusion.
- However, rearranging premises/conclusion incorrectly can lead to invalid reasoning even if all statements are true:    
    - All humans are mortal.
    - Socrates is mortal.        
    - Therefore, Socrates is a human. (Invalid)
- Validity depends on **form** of reasoning, not on truth of individual statements alone.
