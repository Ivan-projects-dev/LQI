#Physics #Quantum 
In quantum mechanics, the **Hamiltonian** is Matrix that represents the total energy of system. It includes both the kinetic energy (motion of particles) & potential energy (interaction forces). Its eigenvalues correspond to the possible energy levels of the system which are "allowed", & the corresponding eigenstates are the quantum states that have definite energy values.

The Hamiltonian $H$ must be a _Hermitian_ operator, meaning: $H†=H$, where $H†$ is the **conjugate transpose/adjoint** of the operator $H$.

This condition ensures that the Hamiltonian has real eigenvalues, which correspond to measurable energy values.
