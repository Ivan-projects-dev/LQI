#Philosophy #Math 
Logic originates from Greek **logos** meaning _word, reason, discourse, law of reasoning_.    
- Logic as a systematic discipline is credited to Aristotle, though he called it _analytics_ rather than logic. The term “logic” was introduced later by Stoics & Peripatetics.
- Related term **logistics** historically meant computational math but now refers to management of material flow. Both share the root implying rationality.
- Logic is the **$T.$ of correct reasoning** or **$T.$ of valid inference**.
- Reasoning (in general) is a process of **info processing**: an input is transformed into an output via an unknown internal process ("black box"). This process may occur in human brains, computers, animals, etc.    
- Correct (valid) reasoning guarantees that **if the premises are true, then the conclusion must also be true**.    
- Logic is concerned only with the **truth-preserving** nature of reasoning, not with the actual truth of premises or the psychological process of thinking.
