#Math 
Sum of convex polygon angles: $\sum = 180^\circ(n - 2)$
**Area formulas:**
- **Square**: $a^2$ 
- **Parallelogram**: $a \cdot h$
- **Triangle**: $\dfrac{ah}{2}$
- **Right triangle**: $\dfrac{ab}{2}$
- **Rhombus**: $\dfrac{d_1 d_2}{2}$
- **Equilateral triangle**: $\dfrac{a^2 \sqrt{3}}{4}$
- **Trapezoid**: $\dfrac{(a + b)h}{2}$
**Sector area:** $S_{\text{sector}} = \dfrac{\pi R^2 \cdot \alpha}{360}$  
**Segment area (chord-defined):** $S_{\text{segment}} = \dfrac{\pi R^2 \cdot \alpha}{360} \pm S_{\triangle}$

**Circle length formulas:**
- $C = 2\pi R$ - **circumference**
- $l = \dfrac{\pi d \cdot \theta}{180^\circ}$ - **arc length**
- $l = \theta R$ - for $\theta$ in **radians**


$a = c \cdot \sin \alpha$  
$b = c \cdot \cos \alpha$  
$c = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$  
$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos \gamma$

$\dfrac{\sin A}{a} = \dfrac{\sin B}{b} = \dfrac{\sin C}{c}$ - **Law of sines**  
$S = \dfrac{ab \cdot \sin \gamma}{2}$ - **Area using sine**  
$S = \sqrt{p(p - a)(p - b)(p - c)}$ - **Heron’s formula**  
$S = \dfrac{abc}{4R}$ - **Area via circumscribed circle**  
$R = \dfrac{abc}{4S}$ - **Radius of circumscribed circle**  
$r = \dfrac{S}{p}$ - **Radius of inscribed circle**  
$R = \dfrac{abc}{4 \sqrt{p(p - a)(p - b)(p - c)}}$

**Chord** - segment whose endpoints both lie on circle  
**Tangent** - line that touches circle at exactly $1$ point
**Angle between vectors $\vec{a}, \vec{b}$**:  $\cos \angle (\vec{a}, \vec{b}) = \dfrac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}| |\vec{b}|}$
$\alpha_n = \dfrac{(n - 2) \cdot 180^\circ}{n}$ - **internal angle of regular polygon**  
**Centre of in- & circle is the same**
**Central angle:** $\dfrac{360^\circ}{n}$  
$R = \dfrac{a_n}{2 \sin \left( \dfrac{180^\circ}{n} \right)}$  
$r = \dfrac{a_n}{2 \tan \left( \dfrac{180^\circ}{n} \right)}$
- **Harmonic mean:** $\dfrac{2xy}{x + y}$
- **Quadratic mean (RMS):** $\sqrt{\dfrac{x^2 + y^2}{2}}$
