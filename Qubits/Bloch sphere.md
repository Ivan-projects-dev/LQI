In **Bloch sphere**, north pole $= 0$ & south $= 1$ (classical values). Any superposition can be represented by point on the sphere (represented by arrow). The closer the end of the arrow to pole the higher the probability the qubit collapses into the classical value assigned to that pole when measured.
