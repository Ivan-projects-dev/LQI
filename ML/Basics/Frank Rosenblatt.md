#Person $(1928 – 1971)$
**Frank Rosenblatt**  was an American psychologist notable in the field of AI.
In **1957** he invented a **[[Perceptron]]** program, on an $IBM 704$ computer at Cornell Aeronautical Laboratory.
Scientists had discovered that brain cells (**Neurons**) receive input from our senses by electrical signals. Neurons, then again, use electrical signals to store info, & to make decisions based on previous input.
Frank had the idea that **[[Perceptron]]** could simulate brain principles, with the ability to learn & make decisions.
